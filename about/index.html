About Ring of Keys

A national community for queer musical theatre artists, built by the artists themselves.

Ring of Keys exists because finding each other should not take a viral hashtag. Today, more than a thousand Keys make up the largest directory of its kind.

Our mission

An artist service organization that fosters community and visibility for musical theatre artists onstage and off, who identify as queer women, transgender, and gender non-conforming.

Ring of Keys · National 501(c)(3) Nonprofit

The Ring of Keys directory. A thousand artists. One community.
Our history

From a hashtag to a nonprofit.

In 2014, a moment in Alison Bechdel's Fun Home, the song "Ring of Keys" sung by young Alison about a butch woman she sees at a diner, became a touchstone for queer musical theatre audiences. Artists started using the hashtag to name themselves and each other.

By 2015, founders Andrea Prestinario, Sara Stevens, Sue Cella, and Ann McCormack had built it into a 501(c)(3). The directory came next, then the Key Change podcast, then Community Nights, then the KEY Project residency.

Ten years on, the work is the same. Help queer musical theatre artists find each other. Help the industry find them.

Our values

What runs through every decision.

These are not posters. They are the test we apply when the work gets hard.

i.

Visibility

Queer artists named, profiled, findable. Self-identified, self-described, self-tagged.

ii.

Safety

Members control their own data, their own outreach. No third-party resale, no surprise exposure.

iii.

Access

Membership is free. Events are free or low-cost. The directory is free to search.

iv.

Community

We hire each other. Refer each other. Cast each other. Root for each other.

The impact

Ten years. One directory. Every region.

The directory is used daily by casting directors, music directors, producers, and educators looking for queer musical theatre artists. The Key Change podcast has been downloaded by listeners in every U.S. state. Community Nights bring 100 to 300 Keys together every month.

Leadership

The Ring is run by working artists.

A volunteer board and a small staff team, all queer musical theatre artists, all still in the work.

A

Andrea Prestinario

Co-founder & Board Chair. Working actor and music director. Helped launch Ring of Keys from the original hashtag in 2014.

S

Sara Stevens

Co-founder & Board Treasurer. Director and producer based in New York. Operations and finance for the organization since 2015.

B

The Board

Seven seats, rotating staggered three-year terms. Open call every spring. All seats are held by Keys.

T

The Team

A small staff plus a wide circle of working Keys who run podcasts, panels, the KEY Project, and Community Nights. Always hiring queer artists first.

Reach

A national org with regional roots.

Keys live in all 50 states. Community Nights are virtual so geography never gates participation. The annual KEY Project residency rotates host cities so different regional communities anchor each year.

Partner casting directors, music directors, and educational institutions use the directory across every U.S. theatrical market: New York, Chicago, Los Angeles, Atlanta, Minneapolis, Seattle, DC, Boston, Houston, and beyond.
