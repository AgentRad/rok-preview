Our Story

From a viral hashtag to a national directory.

It started with a song. In 2014, a moment from Fun Home, the song "Ring of Keys" sung by young Alison about a butch woman she sees at a diner, became a hashtag for queer musical theatre audiences naming themselves.

By 2015, the founders had built it into a 501(c)(3). The directory came next, then the Key Change podcast, Community Nights, and the annual KEY Project residency.

Ten years on, the directory is the largest of its kind, used daily by casting directors, music directors, producers, and educators.

The KEY Project

A residency for new work by queer musical theatre artists.

Each year, six Keys are selected for a two-week new-work development residency. Stipend, housing, producing support, dramaturgy, and a culminating public showcase.

Stipend

Selected Keys receive a working artist stipend for the residency.

Housing

Two weeks of housing in the host city covered by Ring of Keys.

Showcase

A culminating public reading produced by Ring of Keys.

Questions

Common questions.

Is membership really free?

Yes. Ring of Keys is a 501(c)(3) nonprofit and membership is free for any artist who self-identifies as a queer woman, transgender, or gender non-conforming musical theatre artist. We do not sell or share member data, ever.

Who can join? What counts as "musical theatre"?

Performers, music directors, directors, choreographers, stage managers, designers, playwrights, composers, lyricists, dramaturgs, and everyone in between. If you work in musical theatre, onstage or off, you are eligible. We do not gatekeep identity. You define the terms.

How do casting directors use the directory?

Free, no signup required. Casting directors filter by vocal range, pronouns, market, union status, and discipline. Each Key writes their own profile and chooses how they want to be contacted. The directory is used daily by working CDs across the country.

What is the KEY Project?

An annual two-week new-work development residency for six selected Keys. Stipend, housing, producing support, dramaturgy, and a culminating public showcase. Applications open every February.

How can I support Ring of Keys?

Donate (501(c)(3) tax-deductible), sponsor a Community Night, hire Keys, or share the directory with the casting directors and producers in your network. Get in touch to start a conversation.
